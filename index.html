A Year in Action

National Mission

To increase the number of culturally responsible Black engineers who excel academically, succeed professionally, and positively impact the community.

USF Chapter

USF NSBE turns ambition into direction through programs that help students build technical projects, map career pathways, strengthen their professional identity, and bring STEM mentorship back into the community.

A Word From Our President
"Our chapter exists to make sure that every Black engineering student at USF has the community, the preparation, and the opportunity they need to succeed. That's not just a mission, that's a responsibility we take seriously every single day."
Hannah-Camille Rowe '26-'27 President Electrical Engineering '27

Mission in Practice

We don't just talk about impact, we show up. Here's what that looks like in Tampa Bay.

Community Outreach

A Walk for Education

We raised money and distributed over 50 SAT prep books to students in underserved communities, educating families on college pathways and career readiness.

K-12 STEM

Lomax Elementary STEM

Bi-weekly, our members lead hands-on engineering sessions for elementary students, introducing robotics and foundational STEM concepts.

Environmental Service

Sunset Beach Clean-Up

Our chapter rolled up our sleeves for a coastal cleanup at Sunset Beach, demonstrating our commitment to environmental stewardship and public service.

Community Service

Metropolitan Ministries

Members volunteered serving meals, sorting clothing donations, and bringing care directly to families in need across Tampa.

STEM Outreach

USF Engineering Expo

We hosted immersive STEM activities for K-12 visitors at USF's largest annual outreach event, sparking curiosity about engineering careers.

College Access

King High School College Readiness

Our members joined a scholar panel for rising seniors, sharing real guidance on navigating college life, choosing majors, and building toward engineering careers.
